# Neighbors Trailer — CSR Training Curriculum

**Owner:** Trevor (Co-Founder, Neighbors Trailer)
**Purpose:** Structured training program for new Customer Service Representatives focused on outbound calls, inbound calls, and email/chat backup support.
**Format:** 11 modules. Each module = learning objectives + core concepts + critical facts + practice scenarios + mini-quiz. Trainee progresses one module at a time. Final module = certification exam.

---

## How to Use This Curriculum

Trainee opens the Claude Project and types one of:

- `Quiz me on Module 3` — quiz mode for a specific module
- `Roleplay: angry renter, deposit dispute` — roleplay practice

The Project pulls answers from the unified knowledge base file. The curriculum below tells the Project what to teach, in what order, and how to test it.

## Module 1 — Platform Foundations

**Learning objectives**
- Explain what Neighbors Trailer is in one sentence
- Distinguish between Renter and Owner roles
- Walk through the full booking lifecycle from search to payout
- Define core platform terminology

**Core concepts**

Neighbors Trailer is a peer-to-peer trailer rental marketplace. Owners list their trailers; renters book them. NT facilitates payments, identity verification, GPS tracking, insurance (NT Protect), and dispute resolution. NT does not own trailers.

**Booking lifecycle:**
1. Renter searches and finds a trailer
2. Renter requests a booking
3. Renter completes ID verification (Clear) and MVR
4. Owner accepts (or rejects) the booking
5. Pickup address and owner phone shared automatically once accepted
6. Renter picks up trailer; rental "starts"
7. Rental period — renter has the trailer
8. Renter returns trailer; owner "ends" the rental
9. Reviews left, deposit refunded, owner payout issued

**Key terminology**
- **NT Protect / Protection Package** — built-in insurance covering accidental damage during rentals
- **Toll Program** — opt-in system that auto-tracks tolls during rentals
- **My Reservations** — owner-side feature for managing bookings
- **MVR** — Motor Vehicle Record check, required for renters
- **Clear** — third-party ID verification provider
- **Pending booking** — owner has not yet accepted the renter's request

**KB articles to read**
- "Why Rent from Neighbors Trailer?"
- "Why List with Neighbors Trailer?"
- "How It Works" (Owner)
- "How do I book a trailer rental?"

**Practice scenarios**
1. New employee asks: "What's the difference between an owner and a renter?" — explain in 2 sentences each.
2. Friend asks at dinner: "What does Neighbors Trailer actually do? Aren't they just like Turo for trailers?" — give a 30-second pitch.
3. Trainee names every step of the booking lifecycle from memory.

**Mini-quiz**
1. Does Neighbors Trailer own the trailers it rents? (No)
2. Who initiates ID verification — owner or renter? (Renter)
3. When is the pickup address shared with the renter? (After owner accepts the booking)
4. What does NT Protect cover? (Accidental damage during rentals — eligibility rules apply)

## Module 2 — Account & Verification

**Learning objectives**
- Walk renter and owner through account creation
- Explain ID verification (Clear) and what to do when renters refuse it
- Explain MVR and the most common rejection reasons
- Resolve "my account is inactive" questions

**Core concepts**

Renters must complete two verifications before booking: **ID verification via Clear** and an **MVR (Motor Vehicle Record)** check. Both must pass. If either fails, the booking is blocked.

Common Clear refusal reasons we hear: privacy concerns, technical issues, not having proper ID. CSR should explain that Clear is industry-standard, NT does not store ID data, and verification protects both renters and owners.

MVR rejections typically come from: license suspensions, multiple recent moving violations, license expired, license type doesn't qualify. CSR cannot override MVR — escalate.

Account inactive issues are usually one of: unverified email, missing required listing fields (owners), or compliance hold (admin action).

**Critical facts**
- A standard Class C license is sufficient — no CDL required
- Account types can be switched (renter ↔ owner) but not held simultaneously in the same account in some flows — refer to KB
- Email change requires verification of the new address

**KB articles to read**
- "How do I complete ID verification?"
- "What are the requirements to rent a trailer?"
- "Why is my account inactive?"
- "How do I change my email address for my account?"
- Template: "Refusal to use Clear"
- Template: "MVR Booking Rejection"

**Practice scenarios**
1. Renter calls saying "I don't want to use Clear, it feels invasive." — handle.
2. Renter received MVR rejection email. They're upset. Walk them through.
3. Owner says their account is inactive and they don't know why. Triage.

**Mini-quiz**
1. Can a CSR override an MVR rejection? (No — escalate)
2. What is Clear used for? (ID verification for renters)
3. What license is required to rent a standard trailer in TX? (Standard Class C)

## Module 3 — Booking Lifecycle (Renter Side)

**Learning objectives**
- Walk a renter through booking start to finish
- Explain what to do when an owner doesn't respond
- Handle date change requests, trailer switches, and pickup confusion

**Core concepts**

Once a renter selects a trailer and submits a booking request, the owner has time to accept. NT does not have a hard SLA on owner response — encourage renters to message the owner if it's been over 24 hours, and to look at backup options. After acceptance, the pickup address and owner contact info appear automatically on the booking details page.

Renters can request a date change through their booking. The owner must accept. If declined, the renter must cancel under the cancellation policy (see Module 7).

Renters can switch trailers from the same owner — the switch happens via the owner's "My Reservations" feature.

**Critical facts**
- Pickup address shows up automatically after owner acceptance — renters often don't realize and call asking for it
- Booking confirmation is **not instant** — it requires owner acceptance
- Renters cannot cancel via phone, email, or chat — only through their account

**KB articles to read**
- "How do I book a trailer rental?"
- "How long does booking confirmation take?"
- "How Do I Change My Booking Dates?"
- "How do I get the pick-up address?"
- "Can I switch to a different trailer from the same owner?"
- "How do I contact a trailer owner?"

**Practice scenarios**
1. Inbound call: "I booked yesterday and the guy still hasn't accepted. Where's my trailer?" — set expectations, give next steps.
2. Renter wants to push booking dates back two days. Walk them through self-serve.
3. Renter says they can't find the pickup address. Diagnose and resolve.

**Mini-quiz**
1. Can a CSR cancel a booking on behalf of a renter? (No — must be done through the renter's account)
2. When does the pickup address get shared? (Automatically after owner accepts the booking)
3. How does a renter switch to a different trailer from the same owner? (Through the owner's My Reservations feature)

## Module 4 — Booking Lifecycle (Owner Side)

**Learning objectives**
- Walk an owner through accepting a booking, starting a rental, and ending a rental
- Explain listing approval timing and common rejection reasons
- Handle "my listing isn't showing" troubleshooting

**Core concepts**

Owners list trailers with photos, pricing, dimensions, hitch type, and rental requirements. New listings go through approval (typically 24-48 hours, automated + manual review). Common rejection reasons: bad photos, contact info in photos, missing dimensions, formatting issues.

After acceptance, the owner is responsible for two compliance steps:
- **Start the rental** when the renter picks up the trailer
- **End the rental** when the trailer is returned

Failure to start/end rentals on time triggers escalation templates and possible payout deductions. This is one of the most common owner-facing issues.

Owners can block calendar dates, set rental rates, and message renters through the platform.

**Critical facts**
- Listings cannot include phone numbers, addresses, or company names in photos — these will be removed during review
- Owners do **not** install hitches and should **not** quote tow capacity for the renter's vehicle — that's the renter's responsibility
- Calendar block-out is a self-serve feature

**KB articles to read**
- "How Do I List A Trailer for Rent?"
- "Trailer Listing Approval Time"
- "How do I accept a booking?"
- "How do I start a rental from my account?" / "How do I end a rental from my account?"
- "How do I block calendar dates?"
- "How Do I Change My Rental Rates?"
- Template: "How It Works for Trailer Owners"

**Practice scenarios**
1. New owner asks: "I submitted my listing 2 days ago and it's still pending. What's going on?" — explain timing and check causes.
2. Owner calls saying they "forgot to end the rental yesterday." Walk through what's about to happen and how to fix it.
3. Owner: "Why can't I put my company logo on the trailer photos?" — explain policy clearly.

**Mini-quiz**
1. What two compliance actions must owners take during a rental? (Start and End)
2. What's the typical listing approval timeframe? (24-48 hours)
3. Can owners include their phone number in trailer photos? (No)

## Module 5 — Payments, Fees & Payouts

**Learning objectives**
- Explain renter-side fees (rent, platform fee, tax, deposit, protection package)
- Explain owner-side payouts (timing, methods, common failures)
- Resolve "my payout failed" / "my bank info won't save" issues
- Explain the Toll Program

**Core concepts**

Renter payment is charged when the booking is accepted. The deposit is a hold; it's released after the rental closes (3-7 business days). Platform fees, tax, and Protection Package are non-refundable once accepted.

Owner payouts go out after the rental ends successfully. If the bank account isn't connected or fails, the payout is held and the owner is notified via the "Payout Failed" template.

The Toll Program (opt-in for owners) auto-tracks tolls during rentals and bills the renter — saves owners from chasing toll-by-mail bills.

**Critical facts**
- Platform fees, tax, and Protection Package are **non-refundable** once a booking is accepted
- Card is charged at booking acceptance, not at request
- Owner payouts require connected bank info — first failure point in payout issues

- "How do I receive payouts for my rentals?"
- "Bank information won't submit. What should I do?"
- "How Does the Toll Program Work?"
- Template: "Your Payout Failed / Action Required"

## Module 6 — Deposits, Damage Claims & Disputes (HIGH STAKES)

**Learning objectives**
- Explain the full damage claim filing process to an owner
- Explain dispute rights to a renter
- Define what NT Protect / Protection Package covers and excludes
- Handle "I'm being charged for damage I didn't cause" calls without committing to anything

**Core concepts**

When a trailer is returned with damage, the owner can file a damage claim. The owner submits a description, photos, and a repair estimate or invoice. The renter has the right to dispute the deduction. NT reviews disputed claims and makes a final determination.

NT Protect / Protection Package: built-in insurance, automatically applied to eligible bookings, covers accidental damage. Tire damage from blowouts/road debris is **not** covered. Intentional damage and theft due to negligence are **not** covered.

If damage costs exceed the deposit, the owner can pursue additional recovery through NT.

**Critical facts**
- Tire damage is **not** covered by NT Protect
- Owners file claims with photos + description specific to the rental
- Renters dispute deductions through their account
- CSR should never agree with a renter that "the deduction is wrong" — explain the process and the dispute path

**KB articles to read**

- "How can I recover damage costs that exceed the deposit?"
- "How can I dispute a deposit deduction claim?"
- "What is the Protection Package?"
- "How much does the Protection Package cost?"
- "Am I responsible for tire damage?"
- "What types of disputes will Neighbors Trailer handle?"
- Template: "Damage Claim Form"
- Template: "Deposit Deduction Dispute"

**Practice scenarios**

## Module 7 — Extensions, Cancellations & Late Returns

**Learning objectives**
- Explain the cancellation refund schedule from memory
- Explain the early return refund rules — INCLUDING the 80% override
- Walk a renter or owner through extension procedures
- Recognize a late return situation and trigger the right escalation

**Core concepts**

**Cancellation refund schedule (from KB):**
- 72+ hours before rental start: 75% refund
- 48–72 hours: 50% refund
- 24–48 hours: 25% refund
- Less than 24 hours / same-day: No refund
- Platform fees, tax, Protection Package = always non-refundable
- A no-show = renter cancellation without notice = no refund
- Cancellations must be done through the renter's account — phone/email/chat requests don't cancel

**Early return refund:**
- Maximum refundable amount is **80% of the rent for the unused portion** (per Section 20 of Terms of Service)
- A **$25 processing fee** comes out of the OWNER'S payout — NOT from the renter's refund
- Refund only processes if the owner authorizes it BEFORE ending the rental

**⚠️ KNOWN DISCREPANCY:** The help center article currently says 75%. The correct policy is 80%. If a customer cites the 75% number, acknowledge that the help center text needs updating but the policy is 80%. Escalate to internal team if pressed for documentation.

**Extensions:** renters can request extensions through the booking; owners can accept or decline. Owners can also propose extensions to renters. If a renter holds the trailer beyond the booked end time without an approved extension, that's a late return.

**Late returns** trigger a sequence of templates: Late Return Notice → Urgent Notice → Trailer Not Returned by Renter → escalation. CSR should know when each one fires.

**Critical facts to memorize**
- Early return refund = **80%** (NOT 75%)
- $25 fee comes from **owner's payout**, not renter's refund
- Cancellations must be done by the renter, in their account
- "Same-day bookings: no refund" is a frequent friction point

**KB articles to read**
- "What is the cancellation policy?"
- "How do I cancel a booking?"
- "What if I need to cancel due to an emergency?"
- "Will I get a partial refund if I return the trailer early?"
- "What is the refund policy for early return?" (Owner side)
- "How do I extend my rental?" / "How can I extend a rental?"
- Template: "Late Return Notice"
- Template: "Trailer Not Returned by Renter"

**Practice scenarios**
1. Renter calls 23 hours before pickup: "Something came up, I need to cancel and get my money back." — explain options.
2. Renter calls saying they returned the trailer 2 days early and want their money back. Walk through the rules and the 80% override.
3. Owner reports trailer is 6 hours late and renter isn't responding. Walk through escalation steps.
4. Renter cites the help center saying 75%. How do you handle?

**Mini-quiz**
1. What's the refund if a renter cancels 60 hours before rental start? (50%)
2. Is the platform fee refundable on cancellation? (No)
3. What's the maximum refundable amount on early return? (**80%** of rent for unused portion)

5. Can a CSR cancel a booking by phone for a renter who's having trouble logging in? (No — must be done by renter through their account)

## Module 8 — Phone Skills (Inbound & Outbound)

**Learning objectives**
- Open both inbound and outbound calls confidently
- Handle holds, transfers, and closings
- Maintain pace and tone — neither rushed nor rambling
- Apply the "one-sentence response" discipline used in the Vapi voice agent: reactive, no upsells, no option dumps, no filler

**Core concepts**

**Inbound opening:**
> "Thanks for calling Neighbors Trailer, this is [name], how can I help you today?"

**Outbound opening:**
> "Hi, this is [name] with Neighbors Trailer — is this [customer name]? Do you have a few minutes? I'm calling about [specific reason]."

Always state who you are, where you're calling from, and the reason for the call. Don't bury the lead.

**Hold:**
> "Can I put you on a brief hold while I look into that? I'll be back in under two minutes."

Always come back when you said you would, even if to give an update.

**Transfer:**
> "Let me transfer you to [team/person] who can help with that. One moment — I'll stay on the line until they pick up."

**Closing:**
> "Is there anything else I can help you with today? Great — thanks for calling, have a good one."

**Tone discipline:**
- Concise. Don't dump 5 options when 1 will do.
- Reactive. Wait for the customer to react before adding more info.
- No filler. Avoid "um, like, kinda, basically" and "to be honest with you."
- No unsolicited upsells.

**Practice scenarios**
1. Inbound, generic. Open the call, ask the right qualifying question, route the conversation.
2. Outbound to an owner whose listing was flagged for missing dimensions. Open, deliver the message, get them on the path to fix it.
3. Renter asks a question you don't immediately know the answer to. Practice the right "let me look into that" recovery.
4. Customer is rambling. Practice politely steering back to the issue.

**Mini-quiz**
1. What are the three things every outbound call opening must include? (Who you are, where you're from, why you're calling)
2. If you put a customer on hold for "30 seconds" and it's now been 90 seconds, what do you do? (Come back, give an update, set new expectation)
3. Should you lead with a sales pitch on every inbound call? (No)

## Module 9 — De-escalation & When to Escalate

**Learning objectives**
- Spot the early signs of a frustrated customer
- Use empathy without over-apologizing or agreeing the policy is bad
- Apply the "explain the purpose, don't agree the experience is bad" framework
- Know exactly when to escalate vs. handle solo

**Core concepts**

**De-escalation framework (from the live chat bot rubric):**
- Be empathetic but balanced
- Explain the purpose and importance of processes (verification, compliance, etc.) rather than simply agreeing the experience is bad
- Always represent NT in a positive, professional light
- Acknowledge the feeling, then redirect to the path forward

**Bad CSR move:** "Yeah, that's so frustrating, our system is awful, I'm so sorry." — Agrees with the customer's negative framing.

**Good CSR move:** "I hear you — that wasn't the experience we want. The reason verification exists is to protect both renters and owners; let me walk you through how to get past it."

**Escalate when:**
- Customer threatens legal action
- Active payout failure that requires admin tools (not a simple bank info fix)
- Damage claim disputes where the customer is demanding a final answer
- Account suspension or compliance hold inquiries
- Anything involving a specific booking ID where you don't have visibility
- Multi-topic situations that mix billing + damage + escalated tone
- You don't know — when in doubt, escalate

**Don't escalate when:**
- Standard how-to question (any KB-covered topic)
- Cancellation refund explanation
- Listing approval timing
- Toll Program questions
- Deposit timing questions

**Practice scenarios**
1. Renter is shouting that "this whole platform is a scam" because their MVR was rejected. Practice acknowledge → redirect → next step.
2. Owner threatens to "report you to the BBB" because their payout is 6 days late. Decide: handle or escalate?
3. Customer asks a basic question but is using aggressive language. Stay grounded.
4. Customer says "I want to speak to a manager." Practice the response.

**Mini-quiz**
1. Should you agree with a customer who says "your verification process is ridiculous"? (No — acknowledge the frustration, then explain the purpose)
2. Should a CSR handle a customer who threatens legal action? (No — escalate)
3. Should a CSR escalate a question about deposit refund timing? (No — that's a KB answer)

## Module 10 — Critical Policy Knowledge (MUST KNOW EXACTLY)

This module is shorter but every fact must be memorized. The trainee should be quizzed on these until they get them right cold.

**The 12 facts every CSR must know exactly:**

1. **Early return refund max = 80% of rent for unused portion.** Not 75%. The help center is out of date.
2. **The $25 early-return processing fee comes from the OWNER'S payout.** Not from the renter's refund.
3. **Platform fees, tax, and Protection Package are non-refundable** once a booking is accepted.
4. **Cancellation refund schedule:** 72+ hrs = 75% / 48-72 hrs = 50% / 24-48 hrs = 25% / under 24 hrs = 0% / same-day = 0%.
5. **Cancellations must be done by the renter through their account.** Phone/email/chat requests don't cancel a booking.
6. **A no-show = no refund.**
7. **Deposit refund timing: 3-7 business days** after the rental is officially closed.
8. **Tire damage is not covered by NT Protect.**
9. **CSR cannot override an MVR rejection.** Escalate.
10. **CSR cannot cancel a booking on behalf of a renter.** Renter must do it.
11. **Pickup address is shared automatically after owner acceptance** — most "where's my address" calls are renters who haven't checked their booking page.
12. **Phone number policy: do not share NT's phone number unless the customer explicitly asks.** Default to email or live chat.

**Practice**
The trainee will be quizzed on these in random order until they get all 12 right two times in a row. Then they unlock the final exam.

## Module 11 — Final Certification Exam

**Format:** 25 mixed scenarios across all modules. The trainee responds; the Project grades each response and provides a passing score (minimum 21/25 or 84%).

**Scoring rubric:**
- Did the trainee identify the correct policy/procedure?
- Did they communicate it clearly and concisely?
- Did they handle tone appropriately (de-escalation when needed)?
- Did they correctly recognize escalation triggers?
- Did they avoid sharing the phone number unless explicitly asked?
- Did they paraphrase the answer rather than reading verbatim from the KB?

**Sample exam scenarios**
