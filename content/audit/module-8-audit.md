# Audit: Phone Skills (id: module-8, display number 9)

## Current state summary
Three sections covering openings (inbound + outbound), holds & transfers, and closing/tone discipline. This is a soft-skills module, so most of its content isn't policy-checkable against the KB — it's craft. The KB-checkable bits are largely correct.

## ACCURATE — leave alone
- "Inbound opening: 'Thanks for calling Neighbors Trailer, this is [name], how can I help you today?'" — natural and consistent with how staff actually engage in chat tickets.
- "Outbound opening must include three things: name, NT, reason for the call" — sound craft.
- Tone discipline list (Concise / Reactive / No filler / No unsolicited upsells / Don't read verbatim from the KB) — KB itself doesn't push back on any of these. Some Internal entries (e.g., on response-time complaints) reinforce a "report the issue, set expectations, don't promise an ETA" frame that's compatible.
- One-sentence response discipline — solid.
- Hold timing rule ("come back when you said you would") — solid.
- Warm transfers preferred over cold — solid.

## ACCURACY ISSUES
**1. The module assumes phone-first support — KB suggests email/chat is the primary channel.**
- **Lesson says:** Module is titled "Phone Skills" and structures everything around phone calls.
- **KB says:** `[Renter] [booking] Q: I need to contact support about my booking but want a phone call.` — *"Inform them that for documentation purposes and to provide the best assistance, all support must be handled by email."* Also `[Renter] [general] Q: How do I get help or contact customer service?` — *"Direct users to these support options, recommending Live Chat or the Contact Form for fastest response. Phone number is available upon request through Live Chat or contact form."*
- **Recommended fix:** Add an early framing paragraph in section 8-1: *"Channel reality: NT's primary support channels are email and Live Chat. Phone is available on request but not the default. The skills in this module apply equally to chat (where many of the same conversational moves work in writing) — concise opening, reactive answers, set hold expectations explicitly. When you do take phone calls, treat them as exceptions: longer-form, higher-stakes, more documentation needed afterward in writing."*
- **Severity:** Moderate. Trainees who certify on this module thinking phone is the default will be miscalibrated for the actual job.

**2. Section 8-3's "Don't read verbatim from the KB" rule needs a caveat for the AI tutor / scripted policies.**
- **Lesson says:** "Don't read verbatim from the KB. Paraphrase."
- **KB context:** Doesn't push back directly, but several `[Policy]` and `[Internal]` entries are written as exact wording staff should use ("Inform users that…", "Tell the user…"). The "don't read verbatim" rule is mostly true but has exceptions where the KB language IS the script (e.g., the no-multi-booking guidance, the under-25 cutoff messaging).
- **Recommended fix:** Soften: *"Paraphrase from the KB into your own voice — but **flagged Policy entries** (multi-booking warning, under-25 cutoff, etc.) are written as exact guidance and should be delivered close to the wording. The rule is 'sound human, not robotic' — not 'rephrase even when precision matters.'"*
- **Severity:** Minor. Mostly tone-craft.

## CRITICAL GAPS — topic missing from lesson
**1. Documenting communications.**
- **Topic:** Several KB entries (Internal-audience) tell staff to "document all communication attempts" — for late-return triage, abusive-language situations, etc.
- **KB coverage:** `[Internal] [damage-claims] Q: How do I handle a renter who uses abusive language about damage charges?` — *"Document the abusive communication but proceed with normal claim review procedures."* `[Owner] [extension] Q: What should I do if my trailer is not returned on time?` — *"Document all communication attempts."*
- **Why it matters:** Phone calls especially leave less trail than email/chat. Trainees should be habituated to log phone-call substance into the booking record afterward.
- **Suggested addition:** New brief paragraph in section 8-3 (or a new short section): *"After phone calls: log a brief written summary into the booking — what the customer asked, what you committed to, what you escalated. Email/chat threads self-document; phone calls don't unless you do it. This protects both you and future CSRs handling the same booking."*

**2. Handling the "I want a phone call" pushback.**
- **Topic:** When a customer demands a phone call but the channel guidance is email-first.
- **KB coverage:** `[Renter] [booking] Q: I need to contact support about my booking but want a phone call.` — explicit redirect to email.
- **Why it matters:** Phone-Skills module never addresses the meta-question of "what if they refuse to use any other channel?" — common in the job.
- **Suggested addition:** in section 8-1: *"When a customer insists on a phone call: acknowledge, explain that email/chat lets us document and triage faster, ask what they need to discuss, and offer to call back if it's truly call-required (not just preference). Most issues resolve in writing without needing the upgrade to voice."*

## SCENARIO OPPORTUNITIES
**1. Customer who insists on phone over email/chat.**
- **Suggested prompt:** *"A renter messages: 'I don't want to type all this out — can someone just call me?' How do you respond?"*
- **Suggested rubric:**
  - Doesn't refuse outright
  - Asks what they need to discuss in 1-2 sentences (gets the substance into writing first)
  - Offers a callback if the issue genuinely warrants voice

## QUIZ QUESTION OPPORTUNITIES
**1. Primary support channel.**
- **Suggested question + correct answer:**
  > NT's primary support channels are:
  > a) Phone, then email
  > b) **Email and Live Chat (phone available on request)** (correct)
  > c) Phone only
  > d) In-person at NT offices

## Overall recommendation
**Light updates.** The phone-craft material is solid and most of it isn't KB-driven. The only real fixes are: framing phone as one of several channels (not the default), adding the documentation habit, and handling the "I want a call" pushback. ~20 minutes of edits. Module is otherwise fine.
