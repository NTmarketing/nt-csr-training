# Audit: Trailer Basics (id: module-trailer-basics, display number 1)

## Current state summary
Five-section primer on trailer types, hitching/towing terms, why people rent, and basic safety/legal essentials. Aimed at a CSR with zero trailer experience. Lessons are general (not NT-specific procedure); they exist to give vocabulary so other modules' policy talk lands.

## ACCURATE — leave alone
- Trailer-type taxonomy (utility/enclosed/car-hauler/flatbed/dump/horse/boat/motorcycle/tow-dolly) — categories match the listing categories the platform actually uses.
- Ball-hitch sizes 1-7/8", 2", 2-5/16" — matches KB: `[Both] [damage-claims] Q: What should I check before towing a trailer?` ("Verify you are using the correct hitch ball size (1-7/8", 2", or 2-5/16")").
- 4-pin vs 7-pin plug summary — matches the production plug-config article.
- "NT Protect is a damage protection product, not insurance" — matches the live policy disclaimer.
- "Never quote tow capacity for a renter's specific vehicle" — KB never has staff quoting tow capacity; consistent.

## ACCURACY ISSUES
**1. Lesson over-simplifies tire damage as a flat exclusion.**
- **Lesson says:** "It does NOT cover tire damage from blowouts/road debris..." and Q4 says "tire damage is excluded from NT Protect" full stop.
- **KB says:** `[Both] [damage-claims] Q: Who is responsible for tire damage during a rental?` — tire damage is *conditional*. Renter pays for impact/curb/overload/speed-related; **owner** pays when the tire is 3+ years old, defective, poorly maintained, or improperly installed. KB even prescribes the DOT date-code check and a 24-hour evidence window. Also `[Renter] [damage-claims] Q: I had a tire blowout that damaged the trailer fender and side - am I responsible?` reinforces this — a blowout from a worn tire shifts responsibility to the owner.
- **Recommended fix:** rewrite the bullet and Q4 distractor wording. The honest answer is "tire damage isn't auto-covered by NT Protect; responsibility depends on cause — renter pays for impact/speed/overload, owner is on the hook if the tire was 3+ years old or defective." Q4 should test the conditional, not the flat "no."
- **Severity:** Critical. Q4 is in the final-cert pool too — currently rewards a wrong oversimplification.

**2. CDL/Class-C language conflates Texas with "most US states" without source.**
- **Lesson says:** "for standard trailers (under ~10,000 lb GVWR), a regular Class C driver's license is sufficient in Texas and most US states."
- **KB says:** `[Renter] [verification] Q: What is the minimum age to rent a trailer?` and verification entries — KB doesn't make any state-specific CDL claim. NT does verify driver's licenses through Clear/MVR but doesn't issue licensing guidance. The lesson asserts a legal fact about state law that NT doesn't take a position on in production.
- **Recommended fix:** soften to "most renters do not need a CDL for the trailers on this platform; CDL law is state-specific and the renter is responsible for verifying their own state's rules." Avoid telling a CSR to confidently say "no" to a CDL question — the safe answer is to deflect to the renter's local DMV.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Liability concern if a CSR assures someone "no CDL needed" and the renter gets cited.

## CRITICAL GAPS — topic missing from lesson
**Pre-trip safety checklist.**
- **Topic:** A real pre-trip checklist (vehicle, hitch+coupler connection, safety chains, light test, tire pressure, weight distribution, secured load).
- **KB coverage:** `[Both] [damage-claims] Q: What should I check before towing a trailer?` — full ~10-step printable card with sub-checks per system. Also `[Both] [damage-claims] Q: What are the 3 most important trailer safety tips?`
- **Why it matters:** trainees should at minimum know the checklist exists and that NT publishes it as a printable PDF — gives them an answer when a renter calls anxious about a first tow.
- **Suggested addition:** in tb-section-5, add a paragraph or callout: "Neighbors Trailer publishes a Pre-Trip Tow Safety Card (printable PDF). When a first-time renter is nervous, point them to it. The 8 categories: tow vehicle, hitch+coupler, chains+wiring, tires, brakes, load, mirrors, route." One sentence, no need to memorize the full checklist.

**Reality of "trailer brakes" and brake controllers.**
- **Topic:** When a trailer needs a brake controller; what happens if the renter doesn't have one.
- **KB coverage:** `[Renter] [general] Q: Are there vehicle requirements or restrictions for towing?` and several extension entries reference brake-controller mismatches as a real cancellation trigger.
- **Why it matters:** the lesson mentions brake controllers in passing but doesn't address the most common variant — renter shows up to pickup without a brake controller in their truck, the trailer has electric brakes, and they can't legally tow it.
- **Suggested addition:** in tb-section-3, add 1-2 sentences: "If a trailer has electric brakes (typically anything over ~3,000 lb), the tow vehicle needs a brake controller installed. This is the most common reason a renter can't take a trailer they thought they were ready to tow."

## SCENARIO OPPORTUNITIES
- **Real situation pattern from KB:** "Renter calls saying 'the trailer was unsafe / had a tire blowout / I think the tire was old.'" KB has multiple matching entries (renter dispute over tire age, owner dispute over tire damage, the DOT date-code lookup).
- **Why it would make a good scenario:** trains the conditional reasoning that Q4 currently flunks.
- **Suggested prompt:** "A renter calls. They had a tire blowout 3 hours into a rental and the trailer's fender got damaged. The owner is filing a deposit deduction claim. The renter says 'the tire looked super worn, this isn't my fault.' Walk through what you tell them."
- **Suggested rubric:**
  - Doesn't take a side on whether the renter or owner is responsible
  - Mentions the DOT date code check (or directs them to find it)
  - Tells the renter to dispute with photo evidence within 24 hours

## QUIZ QUESTION OPPORTUNITIES
- **Topic:** When tire damage shifts to the owner.
- **Why this should be tested:** correcting the Q4 oversimplification — a CSR who's been certified with the current Q4 will confidently tell a renter "tire damage isn't covered" and create a bad customer outcome.
- **Suggested question + correct answer:**
  > A renter blows a tire and the trailer's fender is damaged. Under what condition would NT typically hold the OWNER responsible (not the renter)?
  > a) If the renter was driving under 55 mph
  > b) If the tire was 3+ years old or showed manufacturer defects (correct)
  > c) If the renter has personal auto insurance
  > d) Owners are never responsible for tire damage

## Overall recommendation
**Light updates.** Module is generally solid as a primer — it correctly avoids overclaiming on policy and it sets up vocabulary well. Two real fixes needed: the tire-damage oversimplification (also fix Q4) and the Class-C/CDL state-specific phrasing. The pre-trip checklist mention is a nice-to-have, not blocking.
