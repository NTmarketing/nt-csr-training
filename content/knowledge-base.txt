--- [Both] [damage-claims] Q: What should I check before towing a trailer? ---
URL: https://neighborstrailer.com/help-center-details/What-should-I-check-before-towing-a-trailer--203
Before towing, take a few minutes to complete this safety checklist. A proper pre-trip inspection can help prevent accidents, equipment damage, and roadside issues.

Download our printable Pre-Trip Tow Safety Card (PDF) for a quick glovebox version of this checklist.

 

1. Verify Your Tow Vehicle

• Confirm your vehicle’s towing capacity is sufficient for the loaded trailer.
• Check that your receiver hitch size matches your ball mount.
• Ensure your vehicle has a brake controller if required.
• Inspect mirrors and adjust for proper rear visibility.
 

2. Confirm Proper Hitch & Coupler Connection

• Verify you are using the correct hitch ball size (1-7/8", 2", or 2-5/16").
• Ensure the hitch ball is properly torqued and tightened.
• Insert the ball mount fully into the receiver and secure it with a hitch pin and clip.
• Open the trailer coupler latch before lowering it onto the hitch ball.
• Lower the coupler completely onto the ball.
• Close and secure the coupler latch.
• Insert a safety pin or coupler lock, if available.
• Perform a gentle pull test to confirm the trailer is securely attached.
 

3. Attach Safety Chains & Wiring

• Cross the safety chains underneath the trailer tongue (forming an “X”).
• Attach chains securely to the tow vehicle.
• Ensure chains have enough slack for turning, but do not drag.
• Connect the trailer light plug.
• Test:• Brake lights
• Turn signals
• Running lights
• Hazard lights

 

4. Inspect Tires & Brakes

• Check tire pressure on both the trailer and tow vehicle.
• Inspect tires for visible damage or excessive wear.
• Confirm lug nuts appear secure.
• Verify trailer brakes are functioning properly (if equipped).
 

5. Check Load & Weight Distribution

• Confirm the trailer is not overloaded (verify GVWR on the VIN sticker).
• Follow the 60/40 rule:• 60% of weight toward the front
• 40% toward the rear

• Ensure cargo is evenly distributed side-to-side.
• Secure all cargo with straps or tie-downs.
• Double-check that nothing can shift during transit.
 

6. Final Walk-Around

• Raise and secure the trailer jack fully.
• Remove wheel chocks.
• Confirm ramps or doors are closed and latched.
• Make sure license plates are visible.
• Perform one last visual inspection before driving away.
 

Pro Tip:
After driving a short distance (5–10 miles), pull over and recheck your hitch connection, straps, and load security.

Taking a few extra minutes before departure can significantly improve safety and reduce the risk of towing-related issues.

Important

--- [Renter] [damage-claims] Q: Am I responsible for tire damage? ---
URL: https://neighborstrailer.com/help-center-details/Am-I-responsible-for-tire-damage--158
Responsibility for tire damage depends on the cause of the damage.

When is the renter responsible?

The renter is responsible if the damage occurred during the rental and was caused by use, including:

• Hitting a curb or road hazard
• A nail or puncture
• Overloading the trailer
• Driving at speeds beyond the tire’s rated limit
• Any impact-related damage
If the damage was caused by how the trailer was operated or loaded during the rental, the renter may be responsible for replacing the tire.

 

When is the trailer owner responsible?

The trailer owner is responsible if the damage was due to normal wear and tear, age, or improper maintenance, including:

• Tire is 3 years old or older
• Tire defects
• Low tread
• Improper installation
Tires should not exceed 3 years of age to help ensure safe towing.

 

How do I check the tire’s age?

You can determine a tire’s age by locating the 4-digit DOT date code on the tire’s sidewall.

• The first two digits represent the week the tire was manufactured.
• The last two digits represent the year of manufacture.
For example:
If the code reads 2522, the tire was manufactured in the 25th week of 2022.

 

Tips to help prevent tire damage

To reduce the risk of tire-related issues during your rental:

• Check tire pressure before departure.
• Inspect tires for visible cracks, bulges, or low tread.
• Avoid overloading the trailer (verify GVWR on the VIN sticker).
• Drive at safe speeds, especially on highways.
• Avoid curbs, potholes, and road debris when possible.
• Recheck tires during long trips or after driving several miles.
Taking a few minutes for a pre-trip inspection can help prevent roadside delays and unexpected expenses.

 

Important

This is a general guideline to help determine responsibility. Our Terms of Use may contain additional language that further define responsibility in specific situations.

If there is a dispute regarding tire damage, Neighbors Trailer will review the circumstances and supporting documentation before making a determination.

--- [Renter] [general] Q: How much weight can my truck tow? ---
URL: https://neighborstrailer.com/help-center-details/How-much-weight-can-my-truck-tow--93
To determine how much weight your truck can safely tow, you’ll need to locate your vehicle’s towing capacity.

You may find this information:

• On a sticker inside the driver-side door
• In your vehicle’s owner’s manual
• On the manufacturer’s website
The quickest way to find it is often to search online using your vehicle’s year, make, model, and engine type, along with the phrase “towing capacity.”

For example:
“2022 Ford F-150 5.0L towing capacity”

Towing capacity can vary based on engine size, axle ratio, trim level, drivetrain, and factory-installed towing packages.

 

Towing vs. Payload — Quick Formula Guide

1. Total Trailer Weight
Trailer Empty Weight + Cargo = Total Trailer Weight
Must be less than or equal to your Towing Capacity

2. Tongue Weight (Typically 10–15%)
Total Trailer Weight × 10–15% = Tongue Weight

3. Total Payload Used
Tongue Weight + Passengers + Truck Bed Cargo = Total Payload Used
Must be less than or equal to your Payload Capacity

Download our Towing Capacity Quick Calculator Card (PDF) to quickly calculate trailer weight, tongue weight, and payload before towing.

Example

If your truck has:

• Towing Capacity: 8,000 lbs
• Payload Capacity: 1,500 lbs
Trailer weight (loaded): 6,000 lbs
Tongue weight (10%): 600 lbs

If you have 350 lbs of passengers and 200 lbs of cargo:

--- [Both] [general] Q: How should I distribute the weight in a trailer? ---

For safe towing, follow the 60/40 rule when loading your trailer:

• Place approximately 60% of the total weight toward the front half of the trailer.
• Place approximately 40% of the total weight toward the rear half of the trailer.

What is tongue weight?

Tongue weight is the downward force the trailer places on the hitch ball of your tow vehicle.

For most trailers, proper tongue weight should be approximately 10–15% of the total loaded trailer weight.

Pro Tip:
Proper tongue weight is critical for safe towing. Too little weight on the tongue can cause trailer sway, while too much weight can overload your vehicle’s rear suspension and reduce steering control. Aim for balanced loading to maintain stability and handling.

Proper weight distribution:

• Improves stability
• Reduces trailer sway
• Increases braking control
• Helps protect your vehicle and trailer
Always secure your load properly and double-check balance before driving. If the trailer begins to sway, safely reduce speed and inspect your load distribution before continuing.

--- [Both] [general] Q: How do I know how much weight the trailer can hold? ---
URL: https://neighborstrailer.com/help-center-details/How-do-I-know-how-much-weight-the-trailer-will-hold--82
A trailer’s weight capacity can be found on the VIN sticker or a label located near the VIN plate. This sticker is typically placed on the trailer tongue or along the frame on the side of the trailer.

On the sticker, you will see the GVWR (Gross Vehicle Weight Rating).

What is GVWR?

GVWR is the maximum total weight the trailer can safely weigh when fully loaded. This includes:

• The trailer itself
• All cargo
• Equipment and accessories

What is payload (weight capacity)?

Payload capacity is the amount of cargo the trailer can safely carry.

You can calculate it using this formula:

GVWR – Empty Trailer Weight = Payload Capacity

This number tells you how much weight you can safely load onto the trailer.

Important:
The weight capacity listed in the online listing may not always reflect the exact manufacturer specifications. Always verify the trailer’s capacity directly on the VIN sticker before loading to ensure safe towing.

Never exceed the manufacturer’s rated weight limits. Overloading can cause trailer sway, tire failure, brake issues, and unsafe towing conditions.

--- [Both] [listing] Q: What size hitch ball do I need to rent a trailer? ---
URL: https://neighborstrailer.com/help-center-details/What-size-ball-will-i-need--81
The required hitch ball size depends on the specific trailer you are renting. Most trailers use one of the following standard ball sizes:

• 1-7/8 inch
• 2 inch
• 2-5/16 inch
You can usually find the required ball size:

• Stamped on the trailer’s coupler
• Listed in the trailer details on the listing page
Always verify the correct ball size before pickup. Using the wrong hitch ball size can create an unsafe connection and may cause the trailer to disconnect while towing.

 

How do I check my hitch ball size?

To confirm your current ball size:

• Look for the size stamped directly on the top of the hitch ball (for example, “2” or “2-5/16”).
• Check the packaging or receipt if the stamping is difficult to read.
• Make sure you are installing the correct ball mount before pickup.
 

Ball Size vs. Receiver Size — What’s the difference?

• Hitch ball size refers to the diameter of the ball that connects to the trailer coupler.
• Receiver size refers to the square opening on your vehicle’s hitch (commonly 1-1/4 inch or 2 inch).
These are two different measurements. The receiver size determines which ball mount fits your vehicle, while the ball size must match the trailer’s coupler.

 

Pro Tip:
Make sure your hitch ball is properly tightened and torqued according to the manufacturer’s specifications. A loose hitch ball can create a dangerous towing situation.

If you’re unsure which size you need, contact the trailer owner through the booking platform before your rental begins.

Greetings,

Thank you for reaching out.

You should always process an extension request or ask the renter to process an extension request prior to the end of the rental period if you think the trailer won't be back when it is due.

If your trailer has not been returned by the end rental date and time, don't panic.  Almost 100% of the time when this happens, it's because the renter forgot to extend or possibly a case of irresponsibility.

If you are unable to get in contact with the renter and the trailer has not been returned on time, here are some steps you can take:

Contact the renter through the Neighbors Trailer message portal and ask when they will be returning the trailer.
If you don't receive a response, try texting the renter.  If you don't have their phone number, we can provide it to you.
If you don't receive a response, contact the renter again through the NT message portal and by phone / text and give a deadline for the renter to contact you. For example, "Your rental was due back at _____ date and time. I have reached out many times and have not heard back from you. I will give you until ___ time today to contact me or I will have to escalate the matter and terminate the rental."
If the renter doesn't contact you by your deadline, reach out in writing again and tell the renter you are now terminating the rental and give them a short deadline to contact you (maybe 1 hour) or you will contact the police and report the trailer stolen.
If you don't hear from the renter by the 2nd deadline, contact the police. 

It's important to note that when you report a trailer as stolen, it becomes a criminal matter, and you should no longer continue charging for the rental.  The laws are different in each state or jurisdiction.  In some states, the law may require you to give a certain amount of notice to the renter of the rental being terminated before they will allow you to report the trailer stolen.

## Timezone / Timestamp Display

The NT platform displays activity timestamps in UTC internally. Owners and renters may see times that appear offset from their local time zone. This is a known platform behavior — the displayed times are not adjustable by the owner, renter, or the support team. There is no timezone setting in user profiles. If an owner asks about changing their timezone or fixing timestamp display, let them know this is a platform-level display setting that cannot be changed by support, and escalate so the technical team is aware.

Do NOT tell users that support can update or change their timezone settings. Support cannot do this.

## CRITICAL: "Profile" Always Means User Account Profile — Never a Listing

ANY time a user mentions "profile" — in any context — they are referring to their **user account profile**, NOT a trailer listing.

This applies to ALL profile-related questions, including but not limited to:
- "Why was my profile rejected?"
- "My profile is pending"
- "Can you help with my profile?"
- "I need to update my profile"
- "My profile photo was rejected"
- "How do I complete my profile?"
- "Something is wrong with my profile"
- "My profile won't submit"
- "Profile status / profile review / profile approval"

A **profile** = the user's account (name, photo, bio, ID verification, bank account).
A **listing** = a specific trailer posted for rent on the platform.
These are completely different things. NEVER answer a profile question with listing information.

### Profile Questions — How to Handle by Intent

**Profile rejected / pending / under review:**
Escalate immediately. Do NOT explain listing rejection reasons. Respond: "Thank you for reaching out. Our team will review your profile status and follow up with you shortly. If you have any questions in the meantime, please visit our Help Center at neighborstrailer.com/help-center."

**Profile photo questions (upload, change, rejected):**
The profile photo is part of the user account, not a listing photo. Escalate if rejected. For update questions, direct them to: app Settings > Profile > edit photo.

**Profile completion / missing fields:**
A complete profile includes: full name, profile photo, bio, ID verification, and connected bank account (for owners). Direct to app Settings > Profile to complete fields.

**ID verification (part of profile):**
ID verification is completed through the CLEAR integration. If a user says their ID verification failed or was rejected, escalate — this is a support team decision, not something the bot can resolve.

**Profile won't save / technical issues:**
Escalate to human support. Do not attempt to troubleshoot profile save errors.

**General rule:** When in doubt about any profile question, escalate. The support team handles all profile-level account decisions.

--- [Renter] [booking] Q: How long does booking confirmation take? How long until the owner accepts? ---
There is no set timeframe for owner acceptance. Owners are encouraged to respond as quickly as possible, but confirmation timing depends on the individual owner. 

Key facts for renters waiting on confirmation:
- Your card is NOT charged while the booking is in pending status — there is only a temporary hold
- You can cancel a pending booking at ANY time from your Bookings page with no penalty
- There is no 12-hour deadline or auto-cancellation for renters
- If the owner accepts, you will receive a confirmation notification
- If you no longer need the trailer, simply cancel the pending booking — the hold on your card will be released automatically

The 12-hour response requirement is an owner policy obligation — it is NOT a renter-facing rule or cancellation window.
